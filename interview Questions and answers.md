# üîµ Intermediate Level ‚Äì 10 Questions

11. What is Sprint Planning?
Answer: A collaborative session to define what can be delivered in a Sprint and how to achieve it.

12. What is a Sprint Review?
Answer: A meeting to inspect the Increment and gather feedback from stakeholders for future improvements.

13. What is a Sprint Retrospective?
Answer: A session to reflect on the Sprint process and identify improvements for the next Sprint.

14. What is Velocity?
Answer: A metric showing how much work the Scrum Team completes per Sprint, usually in story points.

15. How do you prioritize the Product Backlog?
Answer: Using techniques like MoSCoW, Kano, WSJF, ROI-based prioritization.

16. What is a Release Plan?
Answer: A high-level plan for delivering product increments over multiple Sprints.

17. What is Stakeholder Mapping?
Answer: Identifying stakeholders, their influence, and interests to manage engagement effectively.

18. What is a Burndown Chart?
Answer: A graphical representation of remaining work versus time during a Sprint.

19. What is a Product Roadmap?
Answer: A visual plan showing key releases, milestones, and product goals over time.

20. How does a Product Owner collaborate with the Scrum Master?
Answer: By facilitating backlog refinement, supporting Scrum events, and removing impediments affecting product delivery.

# üî¥ Advanced Level ‚Äì 10 Questions

21. What is Empiricism in Scrum?
Answer: Decision-making based on transparency, inspection, and adaptation.

22. How do you handle conflicting stakeholder priorities?
Answer: Through negotiation, business value analysis, and aligning priorities with product vision.

23. Explain the difference between Product Vision and Product Roadmap.
Answer: Vision is the long-term goal; roadmap is the plan to achieve it through releases and Sprints.

24. What is a Minimum Viable Product (MVP)?
Answer: The simplest version of a product that delivers value and allows feedback collection.

25. What are metrics a Product Owner can use to measure product success?
Answer: Customer satisfaction, business value delivered, adoption rate, Sprint goal achievement.

26. What is Technical Debt, and how do you manage it?
Answer: Accumulated shortcuts or suboptimal code; managed through backlog prioritization and planning remediation.

27. How do you ensure value delivery in a Scrum Team?
Answer: By clear backlog prioritization, defining acceptance criteria, and constant feedback from stakeholders.

28. What is a Release Burn-up Chart?
Answer: A chart showing completed work versus total scope to track progress and forecast delivery.

29. How do you handle a Product Backlog item that keeps failing acceptance criteria?
Answer: Analyze root cause, refine requirements, collaborate with Developers, possibly split or re-prioritize the item.

30. What is the difference between outcome-based vs. output-based metrics?
Answer: Outcome metrics measure customer value and impact; output metrics measure work delivered, like story points completed.
